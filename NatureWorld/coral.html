Coral Reefs: Underwater Cities

Coral reefs are highly diverse underwater ecosystems built by coral polyps. They occupy less than 0.1% of the world's ocean area but support 25% of all marine life.

Often called the 'rainforests of the sea,' they protect coastlines from storms and provide food and medicine. They are severely threatened by climate change and pollution.
